Frequently Asked Questions

Chennai is a popular city for property investment, and there are many great localities to choose from. Some of the most popular include Old Mahabalipuram Road (OMR), Perumbakkam, Madippakam, Puzhuthivakkam, Medavakkam, and Sholinganallur. These areas offer excellent connectivity, clean and green surroundings, good schools, colleges, and hospitals. Rohaan Constructions has a number of ongoing projects in these prime locations, offering luxury and affordable apartments for sale.

Some of the most affordable areas to buy a villa in Chennai include Old Mahabalipuram Road (OMR), Perungalathur Pallikaranai, Kelambakkam, Navalur, Mogappair, Tambaram, Medavakkam, Madipakkam, Perumbakkam, Manapakkam, and Sholinganallur. Rohaan Constructions has a number of ongoing projects in these areas, offering a wide range of 2, 3, and 4BHK villas for sale. Our villas are designed to offer the best in terms of luxury and amenities, while still being affordable.

There are many reasons why you should consider buying a plot in Chennai. The city offers excellent connectivity and accessibility, with all major areas and tourist destinations being well-connected by roads, local trains, and the city metro. Chennai is also home to a number of automotive and IT hubs, which offer excellent employment opportunities. In addition, the lower cost of living in Chennai makes it an attractive investment destination.

When buying a property in Chennai, there are a number of factors to consider, including the location, the size of the property, the amenities, the price, and the potential for appreciation. It is also important to do your research and understand the local market before making a purchase.

The best time to buy property in Chennai depends on a number of factors, including the current market conditions and your individual circumstances. However, generally speaking, the best time to buy is when the market is in a buyer's market. This means that there are more properties available than there are buyers, which can lead to lower prices.

The documents required to buy property in Chennai vary depending on the type of property and the seller. However, some of the most common documents include the title deed, the encumbrance certificate, the property tax receipt, and the sale agreement.

There are a number of financing options available for buying property in Chennai, including home loans, bank loans, and loans from non-banking financial institutions (NBFCs). The specific financing options available to you will depend on your individual circumstances.
